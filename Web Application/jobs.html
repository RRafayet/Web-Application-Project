Network Engineer :

Job Reference No: AB198

Salary Range: $40000- $90000 per year (est.)

Description of the position:

We are looking for a network engineer who will be responsible for maintaining and administering our company's computer networks. Your primary duties will include maintenance of computer networks, and other related systems, performing disaster recovery operations, protecting data, software, and hardware from attacks, and replacing faulty network hardware components when necessary. You will also be working closely with the users of our network in order to identify potential issues and fix existing problems.

  1. The Network Engineer is responsible for the coordination, management, implementation and monitoring of client's LAN, WAN and wireless networks.
  2. Perform day to day administration, capacity planning, management and support of network services, and strategic planning for the LAN, WAN and wireless networks.
  3. You will be responsible for preventing unauthorized access to our customer's data and responding to privacy breaches.
  4. In this role, you should be knowledgeable about security frameworks and systems, routing protocols, and network best practices.
  5. Your goal will be to ensure that our customer's technology infrastructure is well designed and protected.

Required Experience:

Essential:

  • BA in computer science, information technology, engineering, or a related field.
  • Five to 10 years of experience in network administration.
  • Should know general-purpose scripting languages such as Python, Ruby, and Perl.
  • Need to be familiar with different types of networks and architectures (including WAN, LAN, SD-WAN, SD-branch, and so on).
  • Must be able to work with automated network functions
  • Ability to explain technical issues to non-technical individuals, and the ability to understand and build a relationship with the clients you work with.

Preferable:

  • Project Management.
  • Time-management skills.
  • Teamwork and Co-operation.

More:

The majority of your training will be done ‘on-the-job’ under the supervision of senior network engineers. You will also have the opportunity to attend in-house training sessions from time to time.Once you have gained a decent amount of experience, you may step up into a lead network engineer position with team leading responsibilities. Eventually, you may advance your technical knowledge even further and begin working as a network architect on major projects.Alternatively, you might decide to branch out and work in a different area of the I.T. industry, such as network analysis, technical project management, I.T. consultancy or third-line support.

Computer Systems Manager :

Job Reference No: AB199

Salary Range: $100,000-$130,000 year (est.)

Description of the position:

The Computer and Information Systems (CIS) Manager will plan, coordinate, and direct computer-related activities by determining the technology needs of the company and making hardware- and software-related decisions to meet those needs.

  1. Collaborate with users, vendors, technicians, and managers to understand and assess computing and system needs and requirements.
  2. Communicate the goals, policies, and procedures of the company to the CIS team; develops plans to implement these needs.
  3. Evaluate current technology use and needs of the company and recommends software and hardware improvements.
  4. Oversees backup, security, and user help systems.
  5. Remain current on advances in technology.

Required Experience:

Essential:

  • Bachelors degree in Computer Science, Programming, or related field required.
  • Related experience preferred.
  • Proficient in Microsoft Office Suite or related software.
  • Thorough understanding of CIS principles, programs, policies, activities, and procedures.
  • Ability to explain technical problems and offer solutions to those without technological training.
  • Excellent organizational skills, with the ability to prioritize work.

Preferable:

  • Analytical skills to collect, interpret, and make decisions based on data.
  • Leadership.
  • Communication.

More:

Computer system managers need to spend the majority of their time in an office, work a minimum of 40 hours a week, and often work evening and weekends to resolve disputes and meet deadlines. Because of short deadlines and small budgets, managers responsible for the technical goals of their organization will be under significant stress. System managers must be able to effectively communicate using emails and the internet, and supervise offsite employees, because more work is performed remotely. These managers are vulnerable to health problems such as back pain, carpal tunnel syndrome, and eyestrain since they spend a majority of their in front of a computer.
